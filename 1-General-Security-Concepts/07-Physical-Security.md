# Physiscal Security
IT professionals need to know both digital and physical security.

### Barricades / Bollards
In order to prevent access to certain areas of a physical place. To channel people through a physical point. Specifically preventing cars. Can also be a security notice to let people know it is a high security area. Can also be used in extreme ways such as with a moat and a bridge.

---

### Access Control Vestibules
When you enter or exit a building, there are also a great extend of physical security such as access control vestibules. 
- Room you must pass through in order to gain access to the rest of the building
- Might be a place where all doors are unlocked, if you open one of the doors, noone else can enter the room while that door is unlocked.
- Or all the doors of the room are unlocked and when you badge in the first door, all the doors become locked and cannot be unlocked while that door is open
- Or one is always locked and one is always locked and when one is open, one is locked `airlock`

---

### Fencing
Very common and something you can easily see it.
- It may not be the best because it is obvious but helps
- Could be a more transparent fence or designed to hide 
- These fences should be robust and strong
- Very secure areas should not be able to climb it either as well

---

### Video Surveillance
Requiring someone to stay in an area and monitor a location to watch for events
- Can replace this with cameras `CCTV` closed circuit television cameras
- These cameras can alarm or alert with events
- Can even read lisence tags or peoples faces
- Record and send back to one single storage point across all cameras

---

### Security Guard & Access Badges
When you need more than just automation or cameras.
- Physical protection for anything that may happen
- Someone sits at the desk or door and validate people coming in
- Usually **Two People** to provide two person integrity or control
- Almost always there is an ID badge as well including picture, name, details and is electronically logged and worn at all times

---

### Lighting
Another way to prevent people from accessing through dark areas.
- More light, usually means more security
- This can also be done through infrared cameras
- Best case though is a well lit area
- Finding the right light is important. Should light up the area and the right angles especially if you are using cameras.

For example in a parking lot, you may have cameras to light up all the cars, and cameras that are able to monitor from the same anglge

---

### Sensors
Sensors help to detect in both light areas and dark areas. Don't need lights.
- Common to see IR in motion detectors when you aren't looking for video but just movement
- Might even use pressure sensors to notice the movement of people or somoene is passing through
- IR is good in limited area, but large areas might include `microwave tech`
- `Microwave` is designed to detect movement over large areas 
- More advanced would be `Ultrasonic` detection which sends signals to look for a reflection of sound waves to see for collision detections or something that will reflect the sound like movement too
