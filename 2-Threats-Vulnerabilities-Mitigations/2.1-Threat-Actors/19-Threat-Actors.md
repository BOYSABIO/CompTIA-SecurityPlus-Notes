# Threat Actors
A **Threat Actor** (or adversary) is **any person, group or entity that intentionally or unintentionally causes harm** to an organization's security

*Who is behind the attack, and why are they doing it?*

---

### Key Things To Know For The Exam
CompTIA focuses on:
1. **Motives** - Why are they attacking?
2. **Resources / Capabilities** - How skilled or well-funded are they?
3. **Intent** - Accidental vs deliberate
4. **Sophistication & persistence** - How long they can stay undetected

---

### Main Categories Of Threat Actors
#### Script Kiddies
- **Skill level**: Low
- **Resources**: Minimal - use pre-made tools or exploits 
- **Motivation**: Curiosity, ego, fun, bragging rights
- **Typical Actions**: Defacing websites, small DDoS attacks, running public exploits 

Unskilled attacker using existing malware or scripts

#### Hacktivists
- **Skill Level**: Moderate to high
- **Resources**: Varied - usually limited funding by organized groups
- **Motivation**: Political or social causes (activism + hacking) 
- **Typical Actions**: Website defacement, data leaks, DDoS for attention
- **Goal**: Spread message, embarrass organizations

Anonymous or similar protest groups

#### Organized Crime
- **Skill Level**: High
- **Resources**: Large - financial backing and coordination
- **Motivation**: Financial gain (most common motive overall)
- **Typical Actions**: Ransomware, credit card theft, fraud identity theft
- **Goal**: Profit

Highly structured group motivated by money

#### Nation-State / APT (Advanced Persistent Threat)
- **skill level**: very high - elite attackers with top resources
- **resources**: government-funded (unlimited time, money expertise)
- **motivation**: espionage, intelligence, sbotage, or geopolitical advantage
- **typical actions**: long-term, stealthy operations (apt compaigns)
- **goal**: national interest, espionage, disruption

Advanced, persistent, long-term access, espionage, state-sponsored

#### Insider Threats
- **skill level**: Varies - can be an admin, employee, or contractor
- **resources**: Authorized access to systems
- **motivation**: Revenge, financial gain, coercion, negligence
- **typical actions**: Stealing data, sabotaging systems, accidental leaks

Types of insiders:
- Malicious insider: Intentional harm
- Neglegent insider: Accidental harm (e.g., clicking phishing email)
- Compromised Insider: Hacked account or blackmailed employee

#### Competitors
- **skill level**: Moderate to high 
- **resources**: Corporate or private sector
- **motivation**: Industrial espionage (steal trade secrets, business plan)
- **typical actions**: Data theft, espionage, sabotage

#### Shadow IT / Accidental Threats
- **Definition**: Employees using unauthorized devices, apps, or services without IT approval
- **Motivation**: Convenience or lack of awareness
- **Risk**: Data leakage, misconfigurations, compliance violations

CompTIA considers them a type of **insider threat** though usually unintential

### Threat Actor Attributes
When identifying or comparing threat actors, think of these attributes:

| Attribute | Meaning | Example | 
| --- | --- | --- |
| Sophistication / Skill level | Technical knowledge, ability to exploit new vulnerabilities | APTs = high, script kiddies = low |
| Resource / Funding | Access to money, tools, and time | Nation-states = extensive |
| Intent / Motivation | Why they act | Money, ideology, revenge |
| Opportunity / Access | How easily they can reach the target | Insider vs external |
| Attack Vector | How they attack | Phishing, malware, physical intrusion |

---

### Common Attack Vectors By Threat Actor
| Threat Actor | Common Techniques |
| --- | --- |
| Script Kiddie | DDoS tools, exploit kits |
| Hacktivist | Defacement, leaks, DDoS |
| Organized Crime | Phishing, ransomware, fraud |
| Nation-State | Zero-days, custom malware, social engineering |
| Insider | Data exfiltration, sabotage |
| Competitor | Espionage, insider recruitment |

---

### How To Defend Against Threat Actors
| Threat Actor | Defense Focus |
| --- | --- |
| Script Kiddies | Patch management, firewall rules |
| Hacktivists | DDoS protection, monitoring, PR readiness |
| Organized Crime | Email filtering, anti-ransomware, user awareness |
| Nation-State | Advanced threat detection, segmentation, intel sharing |
| Insider | Access controls, logging, behavioral monitoring |
| Competitor | Data loss prevention (DLP), legal protections, segmentation |
